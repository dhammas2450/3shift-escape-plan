Setting Up Your First Lead Capture Page

Traffic without capture is like collecting water with a bucket full of holes. Here's how to build your bucket.

🪣 The Leaky Bucket Problem
Imagine you're carrying water from a stream to your house. But your bucket has holes. By the time you get home, all the water is gone. That's what happens when you get traffic but don't capture emails. People visit, then disappear forever. A lead capture page plugs the holes.
🏗️ What Is a Lead Capture Page?

A simple webpage with ONE job: get someone's email address. It has:

  • A headline that promises a specific benefit
  • A subheadline that explains what they'll get
  • An email field where they type their address
  • A button that says what happens next
  • A free gift (lead magnet) delivered after they sign up
1

Choose Your Lead Magnet

What will you give away for free? A PDF guide? A checklist? A video training? A discount code? Pick something your audience desperately wants. Something that solves ONE specific problem fast.

2

Write Your Headline

Use this formula: "How to [DESIRED RESULT] in [TIME FRAME] without [BIGGEST PAIN]." Example: "How to Get Your First 100 Email Subscribers in 30 Days Without Spending a Dime on Ads."

3

Pick a Page Builder

Use simple tools like Systeme.io (free), Mailchimp (free tier), ConvertKit, or Carrd. Don't overthink the design. Clean and simple converts better than fancy and complicated.

4

Add Your Form & Delivery

Connect your email form to deliver the lead magnet automatically. When someone subscribes, they get an email with the download link. Set this up once. It works forever.

5

Test It Yourself

Sign up with your own email. Make sure everything works. The confirmation email arrives. The download link works. The file opens. Test on mobile too. Most traffic is mobile.
